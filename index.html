Sowerby's Botany

James Sowerby (1757–1822) was a British naturalist and illustrator, known for his detailed and vivid illustrations of the natural world, particularly in the field of botany. His most famous work is the 36-volume English Botany, a project that spanned nearly 25 years and included more than 2,000 hand-colored engravings of British plant species. Sowerby was also unique among his peers for his particular attention to cryptogams, which were often overlooked by other illustrators of his time. His work remains a valuable resource for both scientists and historians.

Sowerby’s botanical prints stand out for their scientific accuracy and artistic quality. He worked closely with prominent botanists of his time, such as Sir James Edward Smith, to ensure that the illustrations were not only beautiful but also informative. His contributions helped lay the groundwork for modern botanical illustration, and his prints are still sought after by collectors for their historic and artistic significance.

The prints included here were removed from the original volumes long ago and focus solely on lichens. They were donated to the University of Connecticut to support research in lichenology. Proceeds from the sale of these lithographs will be used to fund the research efforts of UConn students specializing in lichenology.

Despite being over 200 years old, the condition of these hand-colored copper lithographs is truly exceptional. Given their age, it's quite remarkable that they have only minor condition issues. Some pages have developed slight yellowing and minor foxing, and in a few cases, there is evidence of minor water damage. However, these imperfections are relatively subtle and do not detract from the overall beauty or historical significance of the prints.
